LE-EI-TOF Mass Spectrometer

Laser Extraction Electron Ionisation Time-of-Flight instrument designed for in-situ quantification of ³He in lunar regolith at Oceanus Procellarum. A hybrid architecture combining LASMA-LR laser extraction with NIM/JUICE-heritage reflectron TOF mass analysis.

~5.5 kg total instrument mass
~10 W science-mode power
R > 1000 mass resolving power at m/z = 3
1 ppb minimum detectable ³He
2.8 h per sample measurement
Mission objective COP-0-1 Map spatial distribution of ³He across Rima Galilaei, Rima Suess, and Rima Marius
Upstream dependency Sample system + XRF XRF detects high-Ti sites → rover stops → arm delivers 1–5 g regolith to carousel
Downstream interface OBC + Comms 15 kB/s science data → OBC buffer → 512 Mb/lunar-day downlink allocation

Ion measurement chain

step 1 Collection Regolith scooped by rover arm, 1–5 g deposited into carousel position
step 2 Extraction Laser heats grain rims at 0.1 Hz, releasing solar-wind-implanted ³He
step 3 Filtration Cold trap at −80 °C condenses HD⁺ precursors and water; He passes through
step 4 Ionisation 70 eV electrons convert neutral ³He into ³He⁺; efficiency ~5–15%
step 5 Separation Reflectron TOF separates ions by mass; R > 1000 resolves ³He⁺ from HD⁺
step 6 Detection MCP detector and ADC build histogram; ~1700 shots for 100-ion precision

Sensitivity budget — 10 ppb ³He, 10 ng ablated
